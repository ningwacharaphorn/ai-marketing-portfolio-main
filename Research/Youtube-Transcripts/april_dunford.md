## Summary
This episode features April Dunford explaining positioning as the foundational input for B2B SaaS growth (not branding or messaging). Positioning defines **why your product is the best choice** for a **specific** set of customers, relative to the **real alternatives** they consider (including spreadsheets/manual work). April also shares a practical, testable 5-step methodology and emphasizes validating your “positioning thesis” through real sales conversations before scaling.

## Key points (the “positioning-first” workflow)
1. **Define positioning correctly:** Positioning is not branding or messaging—it’s the strategic foundation that defines how your product is the best at delivering value to a well-defined customer set.
2. **Avoid the common failure mode:** Most companies mis-position because they misunderstand competition (ignoring spreadsheets/manual work/status quo) and don’t involve a cross-functional team (sales, product, leadership) in the positioning work.
3. **Use the 5-step methodology:**
   - **Competitive alternatives:** Identify what customers do today to solve the problem.
   - **Differentiated capabilities:** List what you do that alternatives don’t.
   - **Value themes:** For every capability, ask “so what?” until you get to customer value.
   - **Best-fit customers:** Focus on accounts that care most about the unique value only you deliver.
   - **Market category:** Choose the context/category where your value becomes obvious to that customer.
4. **Test positioning through selling:** The most practical validation is turning positioning into a sales pitch and watching real prospect reactions in meetings.
5. **Validate, then scale:** Early on you operate with a “positioning thesis.” After the first wave of customers validates (or corrects) it, you standardize messaging and scale GTM with consistency.

---
**Bottom Line:** Positioning is a set of decisions that makes your product “obviously” the right choice for the right customers. Get the alternatives and value themes right, validate it through real sales conversations, then scale once the thesis is proven.
