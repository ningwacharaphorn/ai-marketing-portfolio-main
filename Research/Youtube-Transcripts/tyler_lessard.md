## Summary
This episode features Tyler Lessard — currently CMO at TechnologyAdvice —speaking in his former role as VP of Marketing & Chief Video Strategist at Vidyard. The content reflects his thinking during his Vidyard tenure, making the case that **asynchronous video messaging is the missing human layer** in modern B2B sales. Before diving into video tactics, Tyler draws on his time at BlackBerry—watching a category leader get dethroned by smartphones—to argue that sellers who ignore behavioral shifts get left behind. The episode covers **why video humanizes the sales process**, how to overcome adoption barriers within a sales team, and practical strategies for using video at every stage of the deal cycle.

## Key points (the "humanity-first" video playbook)
1. **Move beyond the meeting-centric model:** Live meetings are valuable, but sales teams over-rely on them. Asynchronous video fills the gaps between meetings—keeping sellers top-of-mind and the conversation moving without requiring both parties to be available at the same time. (18:42–20:00)
2. **Use video to build real human connection:** Video is not just a content format—it's a tool to humanize the sales process. Prospects who see a face and hear a voice develop trust far faster than through email alone. Using video to introduce team members or explain complex concepts accelerates confidence in the buyer. (13:30–14:15, 36:34–37:45)
3. **Overcome camera-fear as a competitive advantage:**
   - Most reps avoid being on camera—which means those who do stand out immediately.
   - Tools like Vidyard make recording and sending a video as fast as drafting a traditional email.
   - The barrier is psychological, not technical. (14:04–14:20, 28:09–28:20, 41:26–42:06)
4. **Replace documents with screen-recorded walkthroughs:** Instead of sending long PDFs or decks, record a short screen video walking the client through a specific feature or proposal. This makes it easy for your champion to share the message with the broader buying committee—without you in the room. (23:46–25:10, 31:13–32:04)
5. **Prioritize effectiveness over efficiency:** Sellers default to hiding behind the keyboard because it feels efficient. Video forces personalization and costs a few extra minutes—but the return in response rate, meeting show-up rate, and deal velocity makes it the more effective choice. (13:12–13:40)

---
**Bottom Line:** Stop hiding behind the keyboard. Video reveals the human behind the message, and that human connection is what separates forgettable outreach from deals that close. The goal isn't just efficiency—it's effectiveness and personalization at every touchpoint.
