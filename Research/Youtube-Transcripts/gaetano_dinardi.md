## Summary
This episode features Gaetano DiNardi (Growth Advisor & Principal Consultant at Marketing Advice) discussing the fundamental shift in how search traffic functions in the age of AI. DiNardi explains that **traditional SEO — built on high-volume, informational blog content — is effectively collapsing** as LLMs increasingly prioritize summarized answers over website clicks. The episode covers how B2B SaaS teams must rethink visibility, measurement, and content strategy to stay discoverable in an AI-first search landscape.

## Key points (the "AI-first SEO" framework)
1. **The Great Normalization:** The old model of driving traffic via glossary pages, FAQs, and "What is X?" content is becoming obsolete. These pages are easily replaced by AI summaries, resulting in sharply lower ROI for top-of-funnel informational content. (6:12–7:49)
2. **AI as the Discovery Layer:** The new search journey involves users exploring and narrowing down ideas through AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini), with traditional search engines serving only as a secondary verification layer — not the primary discovery channel. (1:03–1:22)
3. **Measuring Success in an AI World:** Tracking must shift from keyword volume to prompt monitoring and brand recommendation frequency. Visibility is increasingly tied to a brand's presence on high-intent, third-party "best of" affiliate sites — not just on-site content. (8:56–16:35)
4. **Strategic Content Adjustments:** To stay relevant, content should adopt the **BLUF model (Bottom Line Up Front)** — placing the most critical information in the first 30% of the text. Definitive, business-grade language is preferred by LLMs over vague or overly academic writing. (21:56–24:06)
5. **Entity Alignment:** Branding and positioning are critical. Companies must ensure their messaging aligns with the "entities" and concepts AI associates with their category. If a brand's language doesn't match its market positioning, it will fail to earn LLM recommendations. (49:08–51:36)

---
**Bottom Line:** The future of B2B SaaS growth will rely heavily on direct and branded traffic. SEO must shift focus from broad, vanity-metric-driven content to high-intent, specific pain points — and brands must actively manage how AI systems understand and recommend them.
