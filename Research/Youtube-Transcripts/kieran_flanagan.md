## Summary
This episode features Dave Gerhardt in conversation with Kieran Flanagan, the SVP of Marketing at HubSpot, discussing the transformative impact of AI on the B2B marketing landscape. The discussion emphasizes moving from the era of traditional, informational content toward actionable AI workflows and changing team structures. Kieran shares insights on how AI is reshaping how teams create, lead, and remain indispensable in a world driven by Large Language Models (LLMs).

## Key points (the “AI-first” marketing workflow)
1. **Shift from information to authority:** The traditional strategy of ranking for informational search terms is being disrupted as buyers use LLMs for direct answers. Marketers must now pivot to creating unique awareness and authority that stands out beyond what AI can generate.
2. **AI as an execution assistant:** AI allows marketers to reduce administrative overhead, moving from "thinking" about tasks to actually "doing" them. It should be used to handle manual work like drafting emails, building presentation decks (e.g., GenSpark), and iterating on active campaigns.
3. **Modern team structures:**The future marketing team and CMO role will likely be smaller but more technically proficient. Agencies are advised to stop offering generic services and instead focus on instrumenting AI agents to automate specific client workflows.
4. **Leadership through clear direction:** Effective leadership in large teams involves providing clear, decisive direction rather than getting bogged down in excessive collaboration. Kieran stresses the importance of "earning your stripes" through hard work and consistent execution.
5. **New literacy through prompting:** Treat prompting as a vital new skill. Use advanced reasoning models (like ChatGPT o3) for complex, multi-step tasks and build custom GPTs to house and automate proprietary frameworks and workflows.

---
**Bottom Line:** B2B marketing is shifting from capturing search traffic to building deep brand authority and AI-automated execution. To stay relevant, marketers must embrace prompting as literacy, move toward smaller technical teams, and use AI agents to handle the manual "grind" of campaign operations.
