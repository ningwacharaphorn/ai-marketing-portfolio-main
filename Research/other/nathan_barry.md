# Career Background: Nathan Barry (Founder @ Kit)

Nathan Barry is a prominent software entrepreneur, author, and the founder of **Kit** (formerly ConvertKit). He is a leading advocate for "Creator-Led Growth," demonstrating how SaaS companies can scale by building authority through high-value media and long-form video content.

### 📚 Other Sources:
- **Newsletter:** [NathanBarry.com](https://nathanbarry.com/) – Deep dives on the "Flywheels" of successful creators and businesses.
- **SaaS Platform:** [Kit.com](https://kit.com/) – The operating system for creators that Nathan scaled to over $40M ARR.
- **Podcast/YouTube:** [The Nathan Barry Show](https://www.youtube.com/@nathanbarry) – Interviews with top-tier founders on building media-driven companies.

### 🚀 Professional Timeline

#### 1. The Design & Education Phase (2010 – 2012)
- **Author & Creator:** Began his career by self-publishing books like *Authority* and *App Design Handbook*, perfecting the art of "teaching everything you know" to build an audience.
- **Authority Building:** Established himself as a thought leader in the creator economy before it was a mainstream term.

#### 2. The SaaS Ascent (2013 – 2023)
- **Founding ConvertKit:** Launched the email marketing platform specifically for professional creators, navigating the "long slow ramp of death" to reach extreme profitability.
- **Media-First Approach:** Focused on content marketing and direct outreach, using his own journey as the primary case study for the product's success.

#### 3. The Media Engine & Rebranding (2024 – Present)
- **Rebrand to Kit:** Led the strategic transformation of the company to Kit, positioning it as the central hub for the creator economy.
- **Systematic Content Production:** Shifted focus to high-production YouTube systems, treating video as a primary business asset to lower CAC and build long-term trust.

### 💡 Notable Background Insights
- **The Media as Infrastructure:** Nathan views a YouTube channel not as a marketing expense, but as a core piece of business infrastructure that compounds in value.
- **Strategic Insight for B2B SaaS:** For SaaS, Nathan emphasizes that "Media is Infrastructure." A YouTube channel isn't a marketing cost; it’s an asset that lowers Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) over time by building a direct, trusted relationship with the audience.
