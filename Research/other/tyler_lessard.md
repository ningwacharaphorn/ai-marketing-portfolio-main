# Career Background: Tyler Lessard (The Video-First B2B Strategist)

Tyler Lessard is a leading authority on video messaging and human-centered B2B marketing, helping sales and marketing teams move beyond automated, text-heavy outreach to build real relationships through video.

### 🚀 Professional Timeline

#### 1. The Engineer Turned Marketer (Early Career)
- **The Practitioner:** Tyler began as a software engineer, spending 10 years at BlackBerry building the smartphone revolution before transitioning into marketing and business development.
- **The Pivot:** He discovered his real strength was translating complex technology into clear value propositions — leading him into marketing leadership roles.

#### 2. The Vidyard Era (2014 – ~2024)
- **The Video Pioneer:** As VP of Marketing and Chief Video Strategist at Vidyard, Tyler spent a decade helping thousands of B2B sales teams humanize their outreach through personalized video messaging.
- **Category Builder:** He established Vidyard as the go-to thought leader in video selling, building the Sales Feed media network to 150k+ engaged followers and developing the "Video-First Sales" methodology.
- **Recognition:** Named top Buyer-Focused B2B Marketer by Demand Gen Report, top 50 Fearless Marketer by Adobe, and Telly award-winning video strategist.

#### 3. The TechnologyAdvice Era (~2024 – Present)
- **The CMO:** Tyler currently serves as Chief Marketing Officer at TechnologyAdvice, a B2B media publisher operating 30+ tech media sites including TechRepublic and EWeek.
- **Outside-In Approach:** He developed the "Outside-In" content methodology — building audiences by prioritizing what buyers care about over what companies want to say.

### 💡 Notable Background Insights
- **The "Human Video" Obsession:** Tyler believes that in a world flooded with automated emails, the human face on video is an unfair advantage. If you're not invoking emotion, you're not doing marketing right.
- **Strategic Insight for B2B SaaS:** For SaaS, Tyler highlights that video is the "trust accelerator" of the modern sales cycle. If your outreach doesn't put a human face behind the message, you aren't just losing responses — you're losing deals to competitors who do.
