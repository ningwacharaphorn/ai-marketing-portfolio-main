**Topic:** Overcoming Positioning Roadblocks (Alignment, Alternatives, Differentiated Value) 
 
**Reference Source:** [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_im-guesting-on-lennys-newsletter-today-activity-7437118092029296640-vQwD)

### 💡 Strategic Takeaways from April Dunford:
April outlines the “hard” positioning roadblocks that stop teams from landing clear, usable positioning—and how to recognize them early. The key insights are:

1. **Misalignment on What You Position Against**
   - Teams often disagree on what prospects truly compare them to in deals.
   - Common failure modes: debating hypothetical competitors and getting distracted by “noisy” competitors that never appear on real shortlists.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* Decide the primary competitive alternative based on real deals, not opinions.

2. **Pessimistic Product Thinking (Trying to Beat Everyone)**
   - Teams try to differentiate against too many theoretical competitors at once.
   - This creates overly broad best-fit customer definitions and ignores the sales team’s reality of why customers actually buy.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* Stop trying to “fix everything.” Fortify where you already win for a specific segment.

3. **Differentiated Value Is Poorly Defined**
   - Many teams anchor value on features prospects don’t understand or on vague, generic outcomes.
   - They also overload positioning with too many themes or confuse “value” with objection handling.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* Translate capabilities into clear value themes buyers care about—and keep it focused.

4. **No Agreement on What Is Being Positioned**
   - Teams fail to decide whether positioning is for a single product, the company, or a portfolio.
   - This creates inconsistent messaging and confusion across marketing and sales.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* Choose the object of positioning (product vs company vs suite) and define how other offerings fit (cross-sell/up-sell).

### 🎯 Summary of April Dunford’s Approach (The Roadblock-First Diagnostic):
Based on the post’s core message:
- **Positioning fails most often at the decision points** (alternatives, value, object of positioning), not at the “write the statement” step.
- **Real positioning requires commitment**: pick the true alternatives, narrow best-fit customers, and define differentiated value in buyer language.
- **Clarity beats completeness**: don’t attempt to be differentiated against everything; be clearly differentiated where it matters most.
