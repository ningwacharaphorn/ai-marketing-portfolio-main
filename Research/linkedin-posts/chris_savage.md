**Topic:** Optimizing for Influence and Recall (Beyond the Click-Based Era)

**Reference Source:** [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cjsavage_everyone-says-optimize-for-clicks-but-activity-7450566585951399936-Hnyj?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADlNuTQBPNCraJN1g4yISwDD0NPF7CSXzR4)

### 💡 Strategic Takeaways from Chris Savage:

Chris’ core insight is that the internet has fundamentally changed; platforms are now designed to keep users in-feed, making "clicks" an unreliable primary signal for growth. Success now depends on influence and memory—creating content that drives branded search and long-term recall rather than immediate traffic.

1. **Stop optimizing for clicks—focus on influence**
   - The click is no longer the primary driver of growth as users stay within native platforms.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* Target the result of influence: being remembered, learned from, and searched for later.

2. **Measure what’s remembered, not just what’s easy**
   - Marketing teams often over-reward measurable metrics (clicks/traffic) while undercounting the content that actually builds demand.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* Look for signals like increased branded search and people mentioning your content weeks after consumption.

3. **Value "Zero-Click" content**
   - Content should stand on its own without requiring a "link in bio" or a click to finish the thought to provide real value.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* Deliver the core idea and utility directly within the platform to maximize its memorable impact.

### 🎯 Summary of Chris Savage’s Approach (Influence-First Content Strategy):
Based on the post’s message:
- **Influence over attribution** wins in a platform-locked internet.
- **Branded search and recall** become the core success metrics for modern teams.
- **Standalone utility** matters more than driving immediate external traffic.
- **Optimizing for memory** ensures that when customers are ready to buy, they come looking for you specifically.
