**Topic:** The Shift from Static Content to Video-First B2B Marketing

**Reference Source:** [VS Biz Design on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/contentmarketing-b2bmarketing-customerengagement-ugcPost-7373359787205459969-aayb)

### 💡 Strategic Takeaways from VS Biz Design regarding Tyler Lessard:

VS Biz Design highlights that Tyler Lessard's perspective (via Vidyard / B2B Revenue Leaders) is critical for anyone running a B2B SaaS content engine. The key insights are:

1. **Content Marketing Has Moved Beyond SEO**
   - The goal is no longer ranking — it's building real relationships with audiences before, during, and after they become customers.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* If your B2B content strategy is still built around ebooks, whitepapers, and gated downloads, you are already behind.

2. **Buyers Live on Video Streams, Podcasts, and Social Media**
   - Static written content is no longer sufficient to meet buyers where they actually spend time.
   - *Strategic Mandate:* YouTube is one of the primary destinations where B2B buyers discover, research, and evaluate solutions — showing up there is no longer optional.

3. **Meeting Customers Where They Are Is the New Priority**
   - The shift is from inbound-only (blogs, SEO) to multi-format, multi-channel presence tailored to how audiences want to engage.
   - Brands that diversify into video streams gain a significant advantage over those stuck in the old playbook.

### 🎯 Summary of Tyler Lessard's Approach (The Video-First Standard):

Based on this clip and the broader B2B marketing discussion:
- **Relationship Over Ranking:** Stop producing content just to rank. If it doesn't build genuine audience trust or meet buyers in their preferred medium, it underperforms.
- **Video as a Primary Channel:** Focus content resources on video-first formats — YouTube, social video, and video messaging — as the direct path to reaching and converting modern B2B buyers.
